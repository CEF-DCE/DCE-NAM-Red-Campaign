# DCE-NAM-Red-Campaign
DCS World DCE Vietnam war on the Red Side Campaign
NAM 1965-74 - This is a semi-historical campaign meant to represent a certain vision of the Vietnam War. This is the North Vietnamese version of the campaign, which promises to be difficult against the US armada. Aircraft and weaponry will evolve throughout this campaign, as will the authorized targets for the US.

Download : See the Release section to the right.

Here is a list of all flyable planes for the red side :

- MiG-15bis/17 : Until a MiG-17 module is available, the MiG-15 is the closest equivalent.
Without radar, RWR, decoys, or missiles, the task will be arduous for the pilots of this aircraft, who will have to rely solely on their agility and air traffic control to try and position themselves to fire canon on the horde of US aircraft that will soon invade North Vietnamese skies.
- MiG-21 : The MiG-21 at our disposal is a much more modern version than the one used by the North Vietnamese Air Force.
To maintain realism, the armament has been limited to first-generation infrared missiles, and the countermeasures have been removed at startup.
If you rearm this aircraft, you will only have access to the older missiles, but you will almost certainly have countermeasures because we cannot disable them. It is therefore up to you to prioritize immersion by not using countermeasures for this campaign and by not activating the RWR (Radar Warning Light).
The preferred interception method at the time relied on guidance from air traffic control.
The missiles you carry have limited performance but represent a significant improvement over the MiG-17's cannons.
This campaign remains a real challenge against US aircraft, even with the MiG-21. Fly low, listen carefully to the air traffic controller's instructions (F10/EWR/ON menu), and do not stay too long in the same area.
MiG-19 : This powerful but unpopular aircraft, not favored by North Vietnamese forces, appeared only late in the war. By selecting the 925th Fighter Wing based at Monchegorsk/Kien An, you'll jump forward in time to 1972 during the Easter Offensive, at the height of the US air campaign.
As with other MiGs, its armament is limited to low-performance infrared missiles, and there are normally no countermeasures or RWRs.
It's up to you whether or not to respect the restrictions of the time.
You'll have to rely primarily on air traffic control and remain extremely cautious. US aircraft are everywhere, and Sparrow missiles are difficult to detect.
This campaign is very challenging and may lead to some frustration... you've been warned ;)
